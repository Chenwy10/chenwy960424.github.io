Biography

I’m currently a student in the Robotics institute (Electrical and Computer Engineering department) of University of Toronto,working with Professor Yu Sun. Specifically, I’m a part of the 3D vision group which is aimed to develop a Structured light (SL)-based 3D scanner for industrial metrology. Before that, I received the Bachelor degree from Department of Computer Science in Huazhong University of Science and Technology in 2018.

My research interests are computer vision, deep learning, and 3D modeling. I am dedicated to improve the level of automation for industrial applications such as defect detection, surface measurement and bin picking. Recently, I’m looking for an Ph.D program in 2021, and if you are interested in working with me, please contact me through email.

Research Experience

Automated high dynamic range 3D optical metrology

AMNL Mar 2020
In this work, we proposed an automated exposure selection-based high dynamic range method. A quantitative metric was designed for HDR-based Structured light 3D measurement to evaluate the phase information in the image. A paper titiled ‘Automatic Exposure Selection based High Dynamic Range 3D Optical Metrology’ has been submitted

Deep-learning based highly-reflective surface measurement with single exposure

AMNL Mar 2019
In this work, we proposed a new method for measuring highly reflective surfaces using only single exposure. A new skip pyramid context aggregation network was developed to enhace single-exposure images while maintaining encoded phase information. A paper titiled ‘Optical Measurement of Highly reflective surfaces from a single exposure’ was published for this work

The construction of a structured light-based 3D scanner

AMNL Sep 2018
Participated in the development of a new structured light-based 3D scanner. My tasks mainly included the calibration of binocular-vision camera system, ellipse detection and 3D point cloud reconstruction. The developed structured light-based 3D scanner prototype provides point clouds with ultra-high accuracy (below 0.01mm) and fast speed (up to 10Hz).

Deep-learning based glass surface defect detection

Huazhong University of Science and technology Feb 2018
Developing a deep-learning based glass surface defect detection system. The morphology processing was first used to locate the defect, then the proposed lightweight CNN, GlassNet was implemented to classify the defects.

3D reconstruction using two lasers

Huazhong University of Science and technology Oct 2017
Calibrated the two-laser system and show corresonding point cloud. This project includes programming to acquire data from the laser and building a 3D display scene based on OpenGL.
